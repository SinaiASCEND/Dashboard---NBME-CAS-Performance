    How to read this dashboard

    • Relative, not absolute. Each figure is the percent of Sinai students who scored statistically lower, about the same, or higher than the national LCME group on that subscore. Sinai's overall means sit at or above national, so a high "Lower" share is a relative soft spot worth a curricular look, not a failing area.
    • Cohort level only. These USMLE reports contain no individual-student data. The dashboard identifies group patterns; it cannot name which students are weak. (Your CAS, shelf, and CBSE files can add that layer — see notes.)
    • Small areas are noisy. NBME cautions that small percentages and small year-to-year changes in small content areas can come from random cohort variation and should not be over-interpreted.
    • Items are integrative. Most USMLE items feed more than one subscore, so a soft spot should not be attributed to a single course. (Course-built CAS exams are the exception — those are course-attributable.)
